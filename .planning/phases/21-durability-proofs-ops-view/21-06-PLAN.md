<objective>
Ship the `/ops` page: a new fourth nav destination that makes "is the queue healthy" a checkable
fact rather than a vibe (OPS-01).

Purpose: `/runs` stays the payroll surface; `/ops` becomes the transport surface (D-09). This
renders **invariant J-1** — `jobs` is transport state, `payroll_runs.status` is the sole business
state machine — in the information architecture and not only in the code. Phases 18 and 19
deliberately kept queue state a *secondary* indicator on `/runs`, which a health strip there would
undo.

Purpose (D-12): every metric renders beside the bound that makes it meaningful — oldest-due-pending
age against the documented worst-case recovery latency derived from the pump cadence, attempts
against `MAX_ATTEMPTS`, depth split `pending` vs `leased`. "Healthy" becomes a comparison the page
performs, not arithmetic the operator does from memory. A traffic-light abstraction was rejected
because a threshold hardcoded today becomes a lie when the cadence changes — which is why the
cadence constant is pinned against the workflow that actually sets it.

Output: a read-only route, a template, a nav item, a router registration, and hermetic tests.
</objective>
