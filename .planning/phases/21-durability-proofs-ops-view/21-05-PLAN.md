<objective>
Build PROOF-03, the one proof with no incumbent coverage: crashing between Resend accepting a send
and the local `sent` commit results in zero second emails, with the persisted `message_id`
asserted byte-identical across both attempts (D-03).

Purpose: ROADMAP criterion 3 and REQUIREMENTS both warn this proof is **vacuous if it passes
against a fake gateway while SEND-01 is unfixed**. SEND-01/02/03 already shipped in Phase 20, so a
naive "did it send successfully" test cannot distinguish "the fix works" from "the fix was never
load-bearing here." D-03 states the teeth exactly: **the persisted `message_id` is byte-identical
across both attempts, exactly one provider call was made, and it carried the same
`Idempotency-Key`.** Those three assertions are the proof; the crash mechanism is not.

Purpose (mechanism): D-03 mandates an injected-seam failure against a real Postgres, not a hard
connection kill. A hard kill proves the same invariant while being nondeterministic under a shared
CI Postgres, and a flaky proof is a proof that eventually gets quarantined.

Output: a new live-Postgres proof carrying the PROOF-03 id, plus publishable mutation evidence.

**Correction applied from cross-AI review (Codex, HIGH — independently verified against live
source).** An earlier draft instructed the executor to expire `jobs.leased_until` by direct SQL and
then observe a "genuine replay". **That replay never happens as scripted.**
`outbound_provider_handoffs.owner_leased_until` is a **separate lease**, recorded at authorization
and checked independently (`app/db/repo/outbound_handoffs.py:181` computes
`owner_leased_until < now() AS owner_expired`; the adopt-UPDATE at `:345` requires
`owner_leased_until < now()`). Expiring the job lease leaves the handoff owner lease unexpired, so
the reclaimed job is refused at the fence and the scenario silently degrades into the refusal case —
while the plan's assertions claim a replay was proven.

The proof is therefore **split into two explicitly-declared halves with predeclared provider-call
counts**. The earlier draft's "assert one or two calls, decide after observing runtime behaviour" is
not a proof contract: a proof whose expected outcome is chosen after seeing the outcome cannot
falsify anything. Each half below states its expected count **in advance**, and the two halves prove
genuinely different things — (A) the app-level fence prevented the second call, (B) the replay
happened and the frozen identity survived it.
</objective>
