<objective>
Publish the phase's evidence where its intended audience will actually find it: a
`docs/DURABILITY-PROOFS.md` linked from the README, carrying one section per proof — claim,
mutation, pasted red, byte-identical revert green, exact re-run command — plus a companion section
stating the accepted residuals (D-07, D-08).

Purpose: "here is the mutation that breaks it, here is the red output" is the single most
differentiating artifact this milestone produces for its primary audience. Burying it in
`.planning/` would waste it. And publishing the boundary next to the claim is itself the
differentiator: a system that states precisely what it does *not* guarantee is making a stronger
claim about the rest, not a weaker one.

Output: the evidence document, the README link, a rot-guard test, and the human verification of the
one `/ops` property no automated test can judge.

**Corrections applied from cross-AI review (Codex, HIGH + MEDIUM).**

1. **This document would have published false evidence.** Plans 21-03 and 21-04 both named the wrong
   expected failing assertion (verified against live source: PROOF-01's mutation reds first at
   `assert claimed.attempts == 1`, not the post-reclaim assertion; PROOF-02's reds first at the
   response-status-set assertion, not the `inbound_events` count). Plans 21-05 and 21-08 were also
   corrected — 21-05 into two declared halves with predeclared provider-call counts, 21-08 into an
   ordered fencing proof plus a separate genuine-contention proof. This plan therefore consumes the
   **observed** reds recorded in those SUMMARYs, and **verifies each published evidence block against
   the SUMMARY and the `MUTATION_TARGETS` entry** rather than transcribing on trust. A document that
   confidently names the wrong assertion is worse than one that names none: it teaches the reader to
   trust a claim that would not survive checking.
2. **"Every command shown runs as written" was unsafe.** The proofs need a real Postgres, and this
   repo's `.env` points at **live Supabase**. Blanket-executing every printed command risks running
   destructive, `ALLOW_DB_RESET`-guarded fixtures against production data. Commands are now split:
   hermetic ones are executed as verification, live-DB ones are **labelled with an explicit
   `DATABASE_URL=<throwaway>` prerequisite and never auto-run**.
</objective>
