<objective>
Fix the remaining ten live-DB failures — the two that red `concurrency-proof.yml`'s first step, and
the eight that **no CI gate currently selects at all**.

Those eight are the more important half. `.github/workflows/concurrency-proof.yml:95` runs
`-m integration` but scopes it to two hard-coded filenames, so eight `integration`-marked tests are
selected by nothing and have been failing unobserved. This is the same blind spot that let Phase 20
sign off a UAT recording `1190 passed, 95 skipped` — an exact match to a run with no database.

Two failures are precisely diagnosed:

- **A1 — `test_concurrent_approvals_exactly_one_wins`.** The stub at `:122` declares
  `lambda rid, run:` while `app/routes/runs.py:424` calls it with `conn=conn`. The resulting
  `TypeError` is caught by the delivery error boundary, the transaction rolls back, the CAS is
  undone, and all 8 threads re-claim — yielding 0 deliveries instead of 1. **Note:** the visible
  symptom is `raise TypeError("run not found")` at `runs.py:422`, which is misleading.
  `repo.load_run` was instrumented and returns `dict(15 cols)` on all 8 calls; it is never None.
- **A2 — `test_a_retry_within_the_same_conversation_updates_the_row_in_place`.** Asserts a retired
  contract at `:259` and `:262`.

Eight are **not** fully diagnosed and are the reason this plan is non-autonomous. Seven live in
`tests/test_atomic_persist.py`, one is `tests/test_ingest.py::test_duplicate_delivery_pipeline_runs_once`.
Bisection at last-green `47c0af0` showed 2 of the atomic_persist failures and the ingest failure
**predate Phase 20** — so those three are not contract drift and may be genuine defects.
</objective>
