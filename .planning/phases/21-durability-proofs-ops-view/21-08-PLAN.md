<objective>
Rewrite PROOF-04's two incumbents so they genuinely race (D-04), and close criterion 4's
falsification half by executing the reclaim-clause mutation live (D-05).

Purpose (why this is the one proof that IS rewritten): D-01 says promote in place and do not
rewrite a working proof. These two are the documented exception — D-04 explicitly mandates their
rewrite because they do not currently satisfy their criterion at all. Everything D-01 protects
still holds: no proof is duplicated into a new canonical file, and the rewrite stays in place in
the file that already owns this surface.

Purpose: RESEARCH.md Finding 3 confirms CONTEXT.md's claim exactly — `test_expired_lease_is_reclaimed`
and `test_zombie_is_fenced_on_BOTH_complete_and_fail` both call `claim_job()` twice sequentially
from the **same test-body thread**, expiring the lease with a direct SQL update in between. They
prove the reclaim SQL predicate; they do not prove concurrency. ROADMAP criterion 4 demands a
"second, genuinely concurrent (real OS thread) worker". This is the exact vacuous-proof class this
repo has already shipped once: Phase 10's CR-01, where threads fired at an `async def` route
serialized and the race never happened, and the test passed even with the safety clause deleted.

Purpose (mechanism, D-04): two real OS threads on separate connections, released by a
`threading.Barrier`, driving the **sync repo seam** — never an HTTP route. Keep expiring
`leased_until` by direct SQL so the proof stays deterministic; no wall-clock sleep and no lowered
lease configuration. Worker B's reclaim and worker A's late complete **and** fail/reschedule must
be driven from their own threads and genuinely contend.

Output: two genuinely-threaded proofs, the PROOF-04 identity on the one covering the whole
criterion, and publishable mutation evidence.
</objective>

  <action>
    Rewrite both incumbents so the second claim happens on a **real second OS thread with its own
    connection**, contending against the original worker's late writes on a third path of
    execution. Preserve everything that already makes them deterministic: enqueue the job, claim it
    on worker A, expire `leased_until` with the existing direct SQL update. Do not sleep, do not
    lower `lease_seconds`, do not route anything through `TestClient`.

    Structure the race using the file's existing idiom. Both threads capture their own connection
    identity, record their arrival, and meet at a shared `threading.Barrier(2, timeout=30)`. After
    the barrier, worker B performs the reclaim and, once its claim has committed, signals a
    `threading.Event`. Worker A waits on that event under a bounded timeout and only then issues
    its late writes.

    Be explicit in the docstring about why the event is there, because it is the difference between
    a rigorous proof and a flaky one: without an enforced happen-before, worker A's `complete_job`
    could legitimately land *before* worker B reclaims — the token is still current until the
    reclaim commits — and the test would assert a fixed outcome on a genuinely non-deterministic
    race. The barrier establishes that both workers are live and contending on the same row; the
    event establishes which one is the zombie. Both are required.

    Assert the criterion's three parts:

    1. **Reclaim** — worker B's `claim_job()` returned a job, and its lease token differs from the
       token worker A holds.
    2. **Both fences** — worker A's `complete_job` with its stale token returned the fenced result,
       and worker A's `fail_job`/reschedule with that same stale token returned the fenced result.
       Criterion 4 calls this second one "the fence people forget"; assert it as its own named
       assertion, not folded into the first.
    3. **Genuine concurrency** — the two threads used distinct connections (compare the captured
       identities) and both recorded passing the barrier. Without these, a future refactor that
       accidentally serialises the threads would leave the proof passing while proving nothing —
       the exact Phase-10 CR-01 regression.

    Also assert the row's final state: the surviving lease token is worker B's, and worker A's
    writes changed neither the job's state nor its `attempts` beyond what the reclaim itself did.

    Surface thread failures: each thread body catches `BaseException` into an error list, and the
    test asserts both error lists are empty before asserting anything else — otherwise a thread
    that died early would leave the real assertions vacuously true.

    Mind the connection-pool budget: `max_size=5`, and this proof uses two threads plus the test
    body, comfortably inside it. State that in the docstring so a future author does not scale the
    thread count past the pool.

    Apply `@pytest.mark.proof(id="PROOF-04")` to the single test that covers the whole of criterion
    4 — the reclaim together with **both** fenced writes. The other rewritten test stays untagged;
    exactly one test may carry each PROOF id. The module-level `pytestmark` already supplies
    `integration` and `queueproof`; do not add the proof marker at module scope.
  </action>
