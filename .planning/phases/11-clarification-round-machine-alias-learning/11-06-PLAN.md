<objective>
Close GAP-2 (CR-2) and GAP-3 (CR-3): a retrigger resets `payroll_runs.clarification_round` to 0 but never touches `email_messages`, so the run's OLD round-0 outbound clarification row (still `sent`) silently suppresses the fresh send (WR-05 reintroduced), and the OLD consumed reply rows are re-accumulated into the retriggered run's extraction context (mispay from a conversation that no longer exists).

Purpose: `email_messages` is the project's append-only audit log (repo.py:47 "the append-only audit log") — the fix cannot delete or mutate historical rows. A per-run `reply_epoch` counter, bumped once per retrigger, gives every round-machine read/write (`get_outbound_for_round`, `load_consumed_replies`, `find_stranded_unconsumed_replies`) a scope boundary that a stale pre-retrigger row can never cross, while every row's full history stays intact and queryable.
Output: `reply_epoch`/`epoch` columns; `clear_reply_context` bumps the epoch; `link_email_to_run` and `insert_email_message` stamp the row's epoch from the run's CURRENT epoch at write time (zero caller changes — both already have `run_id` in scope); the three epoch-consuming reads are scoped to the run's current epoch; two new regression tests proving the retrigger seam genuinely sends fresh and genuinely forgets stale replies.
</objective>
