      actual content and timestamp. A whole-row compare covers all five with no column list to
      maintain, and keeps covering them if someone later widens the SET clause.

      complete mutation surface (necessary AND sufficient), needs no maintenance when the SET
      clause changes, and makes the phrase "byte-for-byte unchanged" literally true rather than an
      overclaim. The deliberately-different subject/body_text on the second write are what give
      the compare teeth: under a clobber they would land on the historical row.

      ONE row, advanced to "sent". This is the other half of the arbiter contract — without it, a
      "just always INSERT" regression would pass Test 1.
